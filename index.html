SM ENTERTAIMENT

History

After graduating from California State University, Northridge in the United States, Lee Soo-man returned to Korea and founded 'SM Studio' in Apgujeong-dong, Gangnam, Seoul in 1989. This business changed its name to S.M. Entertainment and set up its capital fund in February 1995[7] with Jung Hae-ik as CEO.[8] The agency developed an 'in-house' production system and produced a series of successful artists including boy group H.O.T. In 1996, girl group S.E.S. in 1997, Shinhwa in 1998, and R&B duo Fly to the Sky in 1999. Kim Kyung-wook became CEO of the company in 1998.

Introduction

SM Entertainment Co., Ltd. (Korean: SM엔터테인먼트) is a South Korean multinational entertainment agency established in 1995 by Lee Soo-man. It is one of South Korea's largest entertainment companies and has been responsible for fostering and popularizing the careers of many K-pop stars garnering huge global fanbases.The company operates as a record label, talent agency, music production company, event management and concert production company, and music publishing house. SM Entertainment is known for having led the worldwide K-pop phenomenon and the musical side of "Hallyu", also known as the "Korean Wave", with early overseas successes such as H.O.T., S.E.S., and BoA.

ARTIST ACU

SUJU

Super Junior (Korean: 슈퍼주니어; Syupeo Junieo), also known as SJ or SUJU is a South Korean boy band formed in 2005 by producer Lee Soo-man from the record company S.M. Entertainment. Super Junior was also dubbed by the media and the Korean Music Award as the "King of Hallyu Wave" because of their important contribution to the Korean Wave.This group consists of thirteen members at its peak. Super Junior initially debuted with members consisting of leaders Leeteuk, Heechul, Hangeng, Yesung, Kangin, Shindong, Sungmin, Eunhyuk, Donghae, Siwon, Kibum, Ryeowook. Kyuhyun then joined the group in 2006.The fans of the Super Junior band are usually called "ELF" or Ever Lasting Friends. They performed their first debut single "Twins (Knock Out)".

EXO

EXO (Korean: 엑소, often stylized as EXO) is a South Korean-Chinese boy group based in Seoul. This group consists of nine members: Suho, Baekhyun, Chanyeol, D.O., Kai, Sehun, Xiumin, Lay, and Chen. This group was formed by SM Entertainment in 2011 and debuted in 2012. Their music includes genres such as hip hop, contemporary R&B, as well as electronic dance music such as house, trap, and synth-pop. Exo releases and performs music in Korean, Mandarin, and Japanese. The group ranked in the top five most influential celebrities on the Forbes Korea Power Celebrity list every year from 2014 to 2018, and has been dubbed "the world's biggest boyband" and "kings of K-pop" by the media.

NCT

NCT (Hangul: 엔시티, short for Neo Culture Technology) is a South Korean male vocal group formed by SM Entertainment. Introduced in January 2016, the group currently consists of 25 members divided into six different sub-units, namely NCT U, NCT 127, NCT Dream, WayV, NCT DoJaeJung, and NCT Wish. They are famous for their diversity in music, dance and rap vocal skills, as well as powerful appearances, and have been touted as the "most experimental boy band" of K-Pop because (previously) had a concept with an increasing number of members. Until May 2023, NCT has recorded more than 32 million album sales in all sub-units, making them the best-selling artists under SM Entertainment and one of the best-selling K-Pop artists of all time.

AESPA

Aespa (/ˈɛsˌpɑː/ ES-pah; Korean: 에스파; RR: Eseupa, stylized in all lowercase or as æspa) is a South Korean girl group formed by SM Entertainment. The group consists of four members: Karina, Giselle, Winter, and Ningning. The group is known for popularizing the metaverse concept and hyperpop music in K-pop. Aespa debuted on November 17, 2020, with the single "Black Mamba", which achieved the highest number of views in 24 hours for a K-pop group's debut video.

RED VELVET

Red Velvet (Hangul: 레드벨벳; RR: Redeubelbet; Japanese: レッドベルベト; romanization: Reddoberubetto) is a South Korean girl group formed by SM Entertainment in 2014 and consists of five members, namely Irene, Seulgi, Wendy, Joy, and Yeri. They started their career on August 1, 2014 by releasing the digital single "Happiness" with an initial formation of 4 members, Irene, Seulgi, Wendy, and Joy. In March 2015, Red Velvet had a new member, Yeri. The name Red Velvet has a combination of meanings, namely red (red) symbolizes a bright and passionate color, and velvet (silk) symbolizes a feminine and soft image. This duality is reflected in their music and concept.

NEW JEANS

NewJeans (Korean: 뉴진스; RR: Nyujinseu), also known as NJZ (엔제이지; Enjeiji), is a South Korean girl group formed by ADOR, a sub-label of Hybe. The group is composed of five members: Minji, Hanni, Danielle, Haerin, and Hyein. Mainly produced by Min Hee-jin, they are known for their 'girl next door' image and musical stylings reminiscent of the 1990s and 2000s.
